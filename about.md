    <h1>About me</h1>
    <p>Born a week after Jurassic Park was released.</p>
    <p>I wanted to be a movie director. Turns out that involves knowing how to use a camera.</p>
    <p>Smart child. Then I discovered I only liked understanding things, not memorising them. So I studied <a href='https://iopscience.iop.org/article/10.1088/1367-2630/ab7a30'>Physics</a>.</p>
    <p>I write code now. C at the beginning, Java for too long, Go by choice, Pascal by circumstance.</p>
    <p>Ended up as a Research Assistant at the <a href='https://icmplus.neurosurg.cam.ac.uk'>Brain Physics Lab</a>, University of Cambridge. It escalated quickly.</p>
    <p>The physics part is still there. Somewhere.</p>
    <hr>
    <p>I try to understand the problem before liking the solution.</p>
    <p>I separate people from what they say. Not always successfully.</p>
    <p>Identity explains more than arguments. Usually.</p>
    <p>Most disagreements are about belonging, not truth.</p>
    <p>Most problems survive because they are useful to someone.</p>
    <p>Understanding is optional. Consequences are not.</p>
    <p>Simplicity helps. It also lies.</p>
    <hr>
    <p>I say what I think. Then I move on.</p>
